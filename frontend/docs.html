The Problem

SDRs prospecting property management companies spend 25–45 minutes per lead on research that should be automated — rental market health, company news, tenant reviews, property size. At 20 leads/day, most of the workday is gone before anyone picks up the phone.

What It Does

Give it a name, company, and address. In 30–60 seconds it pulls 9 data sources, scores the lead across four dimensions, identifies specific pain points, and writes a cold email grounded in real data.

Score = (Demand×0.30) + (Opportunity×0.30)
      + (Scale×0.20) + (Friction×0.20)

Scoring Weights — click any dimension to expand

Lead Score
0–100
Opportunity 30% — Is there a specific reason to act now?
▾
Driven by news signals (acquisition/growth = 0.95 floor), Google rating pain, and Wikipedia presence. Renter %, vacancy, and walkability are excluded here — they already appear in Demand — so Opportunity only fires when there's a behavioral or reputational signal unique to the company.
Demand 30% — How active is the rental market?
▾
A tight rental market with high walkability means constant inquiry volume — exactly when automation is most valuable. Walk Score ceiling is 80, not 100, so a "Very Walkable" property maxes this component without being penalized against an unreachable ceiling.
Scale 20% — How big is the operational footprint?
▾
A 400-unit apartment complex and a single-family rental are not the same opportunity. Building type scores: Apartment Complex = 1.0, Hotel = 0.80, Commercial/Industrial = 0.75, Office = 0.70, Apt/Shopping Complex = 0.65, Shopping Complex = 0.55, Retail = 0.45, Single Family = 0.20.
Friction 20% — How hard is this property to operate?
▾
High friction = more maintenance calls, more tenant communication, more overhead. High friction is good for us — it means the manager needs automation. Signals: crime rate, precipitation days, snowfall, temperature range, elevation.
